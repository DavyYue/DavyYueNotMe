Ballroom Dancing Doesn’t Have Any Fortnite Moves


Published October 6 2018 • Davy Yue

So, Fortnite came out many fortnights ago, and I swear I’ve seen so many people doing the Floss, Orange Justice, and Electro Shuffle at social parties as well as occasionally in the middle of lecture after finding out they’ve aced a prior exam. To my own regret, I’ve also witnessed a lovely elderly lady attempt the Floss only to look like she was a background dancer to an explicit rap music video. Thank goodness I haven’t seen too many Fornite dances, or I’d have a few brain cells fewer today to write this blog post.


Now let’s get on to the real talk.


The kind of dancing I’m writing about here is the formal type of dancing where a ballroom dance couple drifts across the floor as both a raging stream and calming river in ebbs and flows, rises and falls. My other favorite kind of dance secondary to ballroom is social partner dancing, such as east coast swing in business casual attire. Tertiary would be classic party dancing as seen at Tailgates or BYX parties. But, we’ll focus on the first two types.

In general, both ballroom and social partner dancing has provided me with a positive outlet for a weeks’ worth of stressful exams and difficult homework, as well as a great means to build and fortify incredible self-confidence. Regardless of the convoluted recursive-backtracking problem or a difficult technical-coding interview session, all is well with social dancing at the week’s end. Self-confidence in social dancing originates from not only realizing that “Wow, these beautiful people have a great time dancing with me,” but also that “I look damn fine as a dancer and I love the feeling no matter who says otherwise.” As a domino affect, my heightened self-confidence has propelled me to venture into unknown territory. I serve as the youngest crew member on a board full of tech professionals with 30+ years experience for a minority-led, nonprofit tech conference organization Music City Tech with annual event of over 700+ attendees. In addition, I serve as a VandyHacks logistics organizer for an annual hackathon of 450+ students, perform as a Cafe Con Leche bachata dancer for a 500+ audience, and others.

My college weeks are now spent yearning for the week’s end at a friday evening east-coast swing dancing social at the Global Education Center or saturday night dance at the Nashville Ballroom Company. Throughout the week, I drop by the Vandy Ballroom lessons and popular social partner dance venues close to the Vanderbilt University Campus. Since the summer after my sophomore year, I have now added a total of six dances to my repertoire: east coast swing, salsa, bachata, rumba, waltz, foxtrot, and others to-be determined as I enroll in more lessons. Now that I’m spending my weekends at social dances, I couldn’t be happier.

From my social and ballroom dancing, I’ve learned quite a few lessons as lead, two of which are of utmost importance. A primary lesson would be to never give up, no matter how hard the dance move - unless the move is a dip, then you better as damn well be freaking sure that you keep a perfect record of not dropping any of your lady dance partners. After all, when the beat drops, your follow partner should absolutely not. A secondary lesson involves just having a damn plain old good time.

To accommodate the approximate five hours of weekly commitment to practice and socials with dancing, I have adopted a new studying dogma, which I call the “8-6 business hours.” This studying strategy requires me to attend classes, hit the books, and cry over my assignments all within the hours of 8 am to 6 pm, and only within that strict time-frame of operating hours. However, subscribing to this strict work-ethic requires waking up at the earth-shattering hours of seven in the morning. Yet, a great benefit opens up my evening time of 6:01 pm to midnight - about six hours total - to be more diligent in my extracurricular commitments, regular workout exercises, and, most importantly, dance socials or practices.

I would recommend anyone, even you the occasional reader, to investigate any social or ballroom dancing opportunities as soon as possible if not already; I would advocate starting with east coast swing, in which I myself started this enjoyable adventure. After my sophomore year at the summer’s beginning, I saw myself on the dance floor as a flopping-fiordland penguin, and, by the end of that summer, I had transformed into a graceful king-emperor with some sweet moves to prove it. My metamorphosis happened through a friend’s initial invite to summer weekly Big Band Dances at Centennial Park (Nashville, TN) and successive visits to Fred Astaire Dance Studio for a summer-ending week’s worth of introductory classes. The rest, I can say rest assured, is now history and the present. I very much recommend both of these esteemed events/venues for fine-tuning your body instrument to the sways of jazz for swing for the drum beats of latin music for bachata.

However, I would inform the reader that Fortnite dance moves are banned from any of these premises and performing such indecent acts will result in proper disciplinary action from an elderly Floss-ing lady with her forty pound handbag. Let this be an exercise for the reader.


As usual, thank you so much for reading my story about how I both started and continue enjoying partner dancing. Please stay tuned and hope to see you at my future blog posts. Have a great weekend.
