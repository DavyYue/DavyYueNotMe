First Post: A Creative Writing Piece - The Magician


Published August 08 2018 • Davy Yue

Preface: Hey guys! So I wrote this short essay as part of my Common App profile for college applications, and I kinda fell in love with what I wrote. Also, I need some content to kick off this awesome blog. What better content than straight outta the archives? Hope you enjoy as well!

As the stage lights fade and the velvet curtains rise, the magician strides from a side door to the center stage. Almost every audience member has impatiently waited weeks for this great theatrical performance advertised in bold, commanding words on eye-catching posters and across eager people’s lips. Tingling with anticipation from the crowd’s excitement, the air embraces the night’s series of thrilling festivities to come.

Before beginning the first spectacle, the magician strikes his hands together and summons a hushed silence. From his right pocket, he produces a sheet of printer paper, a mundane staple in every office workplace, and dances his fingers around its edges. After putting on a blindfold, the magician begins manipulating the paper, his hands diligently crafting every fold, calculating every crease, and measuring every surface. In the timespan of less than a minute, the magician conceives an origami swan that can swiftly flap its wings and seems as if it could soar into the skies at any moment. With the following spectacles that go on into the night, he makes each origami model more intricate than the previous, producing among which a fourlayered origami box variation of the Russian Matryoshka dolls, an icosahredron consisting of thirty sonobe units, a beautiful rose which almost blends in with its living counterpart, a delicate Chinese vase for presenting an exquisite bouquet, a braided Christmas wreath to celebrate the seasonal festivities, and a three-dimensional snowflake that dares to melt at any hint of heat, among others.

As the magician himself, I experienced each moment of that evening with excitement, my memories tending to exaggerate with each elated recall. Although occurring in the living room of my own home in front of family and friends, the performance nevertheless embedded itself as a treasured memory and bolstered my pursuit of learning more sophisticated origami models. Studying online origami tutorials and coincidentally meeting some generous strangers who instilled in me their personal origami wisdom, I have accumulated, since the age of ten, expertise in fifteen base folds that serve as generic origami-production blueprints and seven fold techniques for expanding upon said blueprints. Utilizing these fundamentals, I amassed knowledge of how to make a total of twenty-five models, more than half of which I can produce blindfolded.

Yearning to enlighten fellow students with first-hand experience of how origami heightens manual dexterity, improves eye-hand coordination, and enhances visualization of 3D objects in space, I founded the Origami Club during my sophomore year of high school. Within the club, I instruct one or two origami models at every meeting and strive to ensure that all members leave each meeting with full comprehension of how every fold or crease was made. Although I did inspire many members of my club to employ their origami craft in academic projects and for personal entertainment, the Origami Club made the deepest imprints on me, its founder; successive meetings patiently guided me to become a finer teacher, and preliminary advertising requirements coached me on the invaluable nature of humor. Noting that an effective member-recruiting advertisement demands a humorous saying to augment memorability, I devised an origami epigram that has proclaimed the Origami club’s presence whenever mentioned: “Do you even fold, bro?” Within weeks of its conception, other organizations, eager to join in the phrase’s successful dispersion throughout the student body, devised derivatives of the saying, such as “Do you even literature, bro?” In addition through the Origami club, I became an effective teacher through diligent lecture preparation that promotes “eureka!” moments in students; I was enlightened as to how repeated demonstrations encouraged untried approaches to an enigma.

With my talent of many origami models and subsequent invention of the Origami club, my origami skill has embedded itself deep within my identity. I would be more than happy to contribute my origami knowledge to your university through the creation of a club or other avenues if admitted.
